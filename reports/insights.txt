CareMetrics Project - Insights Report

---

1. Key Findings

---

* Peak hospital activity occurs between 9 AM – 12 PM, indicating mid-day congestion
* Consultation and diagnostics stages handle the highest volume of patients
* Average waiting time increases significantly during peak hours, affecting patient experience

---

2. Bottleneck Analysis

---

* Diagnostics stage has the highest delay among all stages, making it the primary bottleneck
* Consultation queues increase during peak hours, causing cascading delays
* Billing stage shows relatively stable and lower delays compared to other stages

---

3. Capacity Insights

---

* Patient events are unevenly distributed across time, with heavy clustering during peak hours
* Staff workload is significantly higher during mid-day, indicating imbalance in resource allocation
* Off-peak hours show underutilization of hospital resources

---

4. Recommendations

---

* Increase staffing during peak hours (9 AM – 12 PM) to handle higher patient load
* Improve efficiency in diagnostics to reduce major bottlenecks
* Implement better queue management during consultation stage
* Redistribute appointments to balance workload across the day

---

5. Conclusion

---

The analysis highlights key inefficiencies in hospital workflow, particularly during peak hours and diagnostic stages.
Addressing these bottlenecks can significantly reduce patient waiting time and improve overall operational efficiency.
